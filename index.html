FAQ

Common questions

How is PainRadar different from just searching Reddit manually?
Manual Reddit searching is slow, biased by what you search for, and misses patterns across posts. PainRadar scans 4 platforms in parallel, ranks every signal by emotional intensity, clusters related complaints, and delivers the full picture in 60 seconds instead of hours. Plus you get Twitter, Hacker News, and G2 data you'd realistically never check manually.
How exactly does the pain scoring algorithm work?
Each signal scores 1–100 based on: emotional intensity of language used, repetition frequency across posts, upvote/engagement signals, comment depth, and cross-platform validation. A pain appearing on Reddit, G2, AND Twitter simultaneously scores significantly higher than one on a single platform.
Is the data actually real? Where does it come from?
Yes. Every pain point includes the actual original quote and a direct link to the source post. We pull from Reddit's API, Twitter's API, Hacker News's Algolia API, and G2's public review data. You can click through and verify every single insight yourself.
Who is this built for?
Indie hackers finding their first idea. Startup founders validating before they build. Product teams researching what features to prioritize. Marketing agencies doing audience research for clients. Venture studios running parallel idea validation sprints.
Can I cancel anytime?
Yes. One click, no questions asked, no hoops to jump through. You keep full access until the end of your current billing period. We don't believe in friction-based retention.
